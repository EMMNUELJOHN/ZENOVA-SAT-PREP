SAT Math Strategies That Win

Knowing the formulas is half the battle. Knowing how to use them under pressure is what separates a 600 from an 800.

‚è±Ô∏è Time Management

  • Never spend more than 90 seconds on any single question before moving on.
  • Do a first pass: answer easy questions, flag harder ones to return to later.
  • Save 5 minutes at the end of each module to review flagged questions.
  • For 22-question modules, aim for ~75 seconds per question on average.
  • If stuck, eliminate wrong answers and make an educated guess ‚Äî no penalty for wrong answers on digital SAT.
  • Use your calculator efficiently: set it up for the test, don't do mental math you can delegate.

üéØ Guessing Strategy

  • Always guess ‚Äî the digital SAT has no guessing penalty.
  • Never leave a question blank. Even a random guess gives you a 25% chance.
  • Eliminate 1-2 wrong answers first to boost your odds to 33% or 50%.
  • Look for answer traps: answers that look right but use wrong operations.
  • If answers are close together numerically, re-check your arithmetic carefully.
  • Between two remaining answers, always pick the simpler-looking one for estimation.

üî¢ Calculator Strategy

  • Use Desmos to solve systems of equations visually ‚Äî plot both lines and find intersection.
  • Use Desmos to graph quadratics ‚Äî find roots, vertex, and axis of symmetry instantly.
  • Store intermediate values in memory to avoid re-typing long numbers.
  • Don't use the calculator for simple arithmetic ‚Äî it wastes time.
  • Know when NOT to use the calculator ‚Äî algebraic manipulation is often faster.
  • Practice with Desmos before test day so it feels natural under exam conditions.

üí™ Hard Question Approach

  • Read the question twice before looking at answers ‚Äî understand what's actually being asked.
  • Work backwards from the answer choices for complex word problems.
  • Draw diagrams for geometry problems ‚Äî visualizing always helps.
  • For algebraic word problems, assign variables and build equations systematically.
  • Use substitution: plug simple numbers (0, 1, 2) to test relationships.
  • Hard questions often have a simpler solution ‚Äî look for elegant approaches before brute force.

ü™§ Avoid the Traps

  • SAT trap answers are often the result of common calculation errors ‚Äî be suspicious of "obvious" answers.
  • Watch for "not" in questions: "which is NOT true?" changes everything.
  • Unit traps: answer in meters but the question asks for centimeters.
  • Read carefully: "at least," "at most," "greater than," "no less than" each have different meanings.
  • Coordinate plane traps: don't confuse x and y intercepts.
  • Percent traps: "30% more than" is different from "30% of" ‚Äî know the difference.

üìñ Day-Before & Test Day

  • Don't cram the night before ‚Äî review formulas lightly, then rest well.
  • Eat a solid breakfast: your brain needs fuel for 2+ hours of testing.
  • Arrive early ‚Äî familiar surroundings reduce anxiety.
  • Bring your calculator fully charged (if you have a physical one).
  • Read every question word for word ‚Äî rushing causes careless errors.
  • Stay calm: if a question stumps you, breathe, skip it, and come back.

üìù Reading & Writing Strategy

  • Read the question first ‚Äî then find the answer in the passage, not the other way around.
  • For grammar questions, read the full sentence before choosing punctuation.
  • Transitions ("however," "therefore," "moreover") ‚Äî know what each one implies logically.
  • Main idea questions: ignore specific details and focus on what the whole passage is about.
  • Vocabulary in context: re-read 2 sentences before and after the word.
  • For evidence questions, always link your answer to what the passage literally says.

üîç Process of Elimination

  • Cross out choices that are clearly wrong first ‚Äî you'll narrow down faster.
  • If two choices seem similar, find what makes them different ‚Äî that's the key to choosing.
  • Extreme language ("always," "never," "all") is usually wrong on reading questions.
  • For math, plug in values to eliminate answer choices that don't work.
  • Distractor answers on the SAT are often half-right ‚Äî read every word carefully.
  • If you eliminate 3 choices, go with the remaining one even if you're unsure.
